Henry county, a county in Southern metro Atlanta, had received an F grade from the AlA in air quality and was ranked the 22nd most ozone polluted county in the nation. The Lung Association has a color-coded system to determine the amount of ozone in counties: an “orange” day meant it was unhealthy for sensitive groups, a “red” day was unhealthy for the general population, and a “purple” day was unhealthy for all people. Henry county had 44 “orange” days, 11 “red” days, and five “purple” days from 1999 to 2001.
