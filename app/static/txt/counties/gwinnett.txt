Gwinnett county, north of Atlanta, is 52.5% white, 24.2% African American, 10.8% Asian, and 0.3% American Indian, Pacific Islander, multiracial or unknown, is one of the most diverse and largest counties in all of america. Its tremendous population size means that environmental concerns in the area are even more closely watched, but with its extensive diversity, this “watching” doesn’t always come fairly. Gwinnett county has 4 major environmental justice hotspots according to Greenlaw. Three out of four are located towards the city of Norcross, one of the poorest areas in the county, that is predominantly hispanic.
