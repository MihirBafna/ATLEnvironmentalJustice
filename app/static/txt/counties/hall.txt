Hall county, located northeast of Atlanta, has a history of hispanic immigration and minority underepresentation. This underepresentation leads majority groups disregarding environmental concerns that are located in closest proximity to minority groups. In fact, there was recently a minority community built on top of a previous landfill . This location put these people at risk to negative health affects as this land has been exposed to a tremendous amount of harmful chemicals and substances. In additions, Hall county has large concerns to focus on related to water pollution, as the county is home of Lake Sydney Lanier, the largest lake in Georgia. Other than often goes through droughts the local governments are actively attempting to monitor the amount of toxic waste that goes into the man-made lake.
